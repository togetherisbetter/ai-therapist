Who is this for

     A core element of long-term individual therapy is the setting — a clear agreement between client and therapist about when, where, and how the work happens. That includes the time and frequency of sessions, duration, payment, and the ethical and technical boundaries that create safety and structure.

    But in real life, many people find themselves in situations where a stable therapy setting simply isn’t possible. They may not be able to:

  • consistently carve out a full hour just for themselves,
  • speak out loud or join a video call,
  • ensure they won’t be interrupted or overheard,
  • commit financially to ongoing therapy,
  • or even know when they’ll next be free.

This includes, for example:

  • new mothers caring for infants,
  • sailors on months-long voyages at sea,
  • oil rig workers with unpredictable shifts,
  • students living in shared dorms,
  • pilots and flight attendants constantly changing time zones,
  • and many others.

    They often need support — but don’t have access to traditional therapy formats. That’s why we built this one.

About supervision

    Today, many people experiment with using generative AI chat models (like ChatGPT) for therapeutic conversations. But these general-purpose models are trained on broad internet data, not on the principles or structure of real psychotherapy.

    Without careful design and oversight, these chats can be misleading or even harmful, especially when someone is vulnerable and looking for real support.

    That’s why we’re building something different.

    Our chatbot is trained specifically for therapeutic interaction — with a focus on empathy, safety, and psychological depth. And it doesn’t operate alone: all conversations and improvements are regularly supervised by professional psychologists.

    Supervision ensures that the AI stays grounded in real therapeutic values — and that the support you receive is as responsible as it is accessible.
